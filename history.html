STORIA IN ITALIANO

History

In the 1960s, companies located on the western branch of Lake Como began to face the first problems due to the increasingly frequent navigation and the wind that made the beautiful waters of Lake Como increasingly rough.
Alternatives were sought in smaller lakes, created by the same glacier that generated Lake Como, which, placed in a semicircle, bring the Lecco branch closer to that of Como. Among these, Lake Pusiano represents the best solution both from a meteorological point of view, thanks to an almost total absence of wind, and from the point of view of the tranquility of the lake, since navigation is prohibited. The tranquility of the waters is combined with a length, width, and overall surface area that are very suitable for training, technique, and training activities for rowing.

The first companies therefore appear, which subsequently in the 1980s invite the National Rowing Federation to consider the opportunity to finance a peripheral headquarters essential for the competitive activity of the companies of Lake Como. These are the last years of a great and beloved Federal President who is very sensitive to the development of rowing and does not miss the opportunity to give importance to rowing.
It was Paolo D'Aloja who personally brought the funds for the construction of the first boat hangar to the then mayor of Eupilio, Vittorio Molteni.

Thus began the history of the Rowing Center which was subsequently expanded in the 1990s, adding a small gym with a rowing room and a sports medicine center.
The companies of Lake Como moved their operational headquarters for the competitive preparation of their teams to Lake Pusiano. They created a committee and began to collaborate on the management of the Center. The first and only aggregation project in Italy.
This brings us to 2010 when, in a beautiful evening at the Canottieri Lario restaurant, the sports councilor of the province of Como, Achille Moioli, presented the lake companies with a feasibility study to expand and complete the center which had become insufficient to accommodate all the athletes of the Lake.

From the incredulity of the companies, they soon moved on to surprise when they saw the proposal realized in a preliminary project. The realization was entrusted to the Lario Consortium, now the Lake and Minor Lakes Basin Authority. From the project, they moved on to the request for financing, which involved, in addition to the provinces of Como and Lecco, 7 municipalities and as many environmental protection and territorial protection entities and associations. Everyone believes in it, everyone is committed. They reach the assignment of the works and the laying of the first stone; in November 2012, work began on the construction of what we can define today as one of the most beautiful rowing centers in Italy.
